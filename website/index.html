What is NoneReceipt?

In the retail sector, it is common for cash registers and credit card terminals to automatically print a receipt when the transaction is completed. Merchants do not have to show what happens to the printed receipts: customers refuse to accept them, leave them at the till on departure, throw them in the nearest bin or, in the worst case, leave the receipts on the shop floor.

Today's receipts are redundant, hardly fulfilling their real role. They are inconvenient to use, short-lived, harmful to the environment and human health, and expensive.

NoneReceipt offers a solution to this problem.

With our software, businesses don't have to deal with printing receipts when making purchases. The digitised receipt is immediately displayed in the customer's application, so they can continue to easily track their purchase history while protecting the environment.

In the United States alone, the paper needed to produce receipts results in the cutting down of more than 3 million trees, the use of 34 billion litres of water and the equivalent of 400,000 cars emitting carbon dioxide every year. This is compounded by the fact that thermal paper cannot be recycled because of the chemicals it contains, resulting in 700,000 tonnes of toxic waste in the US every year.

It is a little known fact that a significant proportion (estimated at 93%) of the thermal paper used as the basis for receipts contains Bisphenol A (BpA) or Bisphenol S (BpS). These are monomers or precursors used in the production of plastics. They are responsible for the legibility of printed text in thermal paper. In recent years, however, a number of studies have shown that they can be the cause of a number of health problems, including cancer, type 2 diabetes, thyroid disorders, obesity, infertility, and birth or developmental defects.

Bisphenol A does not always cause an immediate reaction on contact with the receipt (some sensitive individuals may experience skin or eye irritation), is absorbed through the skin and accumulates in the body. However, in the body, it starts to act as an endocrine disruptor, i.e. it increases the levels of some hormones and decreases the levels of others, linked to the endocrine system. In the long term, this change can cause serious abnormalities and, in pregnant women, impaired foetal development.
